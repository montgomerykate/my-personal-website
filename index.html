Step 1: Time and Money

Road tripping is by far, one of the most cost effective ways to see and experience so many places in succession with one another. And the very best starting spot for finding out what you can and cannot do is to simply knowing how much time you have and how much money you're willing to spend.

Start by picking your days, whether you want to go for 5 days or 3 months, having a time frame for everything you want to do is detrimental to everything. For the sake of the example, I'm just going to say the trip will be 2 weeks.

Another very important item is insuring that you set your budget and make sure that when you're doing the math to add up expenses you are shooting high! It's better to come home under budget than spending hundreds more than you thought you would!! (Also, again, for the sake of example, I will be setting the budget to $2,000

Google is your best friend! Want to check gas prices across the country? Google it. Find cool things to do you've never heard of? Google. Hotels? Park information? Campsites? Rental cars? Free camping?? All Google!!

I find that the best way to organze expenses and to help budget properly is to make a table. A very smart way to save money on the road is to buy loads of foods that dont need to be refridgerated and can be easily accessed while driving. This also will allow you more driving time and in turn allow you to see more!

Name Cost of expense Left in budget
Gas $400 $1,600

Souvineers $250 $850
Food $400 $450

Notice how there is money left over in the innital budget? Thats there for a reason. Sometimes emergencies happen and you might have to pay to replace a tire, or might cut your hand and need stitches. Things happen so it's always good to be prepared for the worst. The road trip motto should always be "prepare for the worst and hope for the best" just so you have all of your bases covered.

Step 2: Mapping your route.

As much as I emphasized it in the previous section, Google is your best friend! Find a ton os places you want to go and print yourself out a map and make marks of all the places your want to go. Having the visual of all the places and their distance from one another can really help with planning and making decisions on extra places to go or certain places to cut out of the trip to save for next time!

Step 3: Find what kind of road tripping experiences you want.

Do you want to see cities or nature? Hot or cold climates? Luxury? Or roughing it? Van life or SUV? There is simply, no wrong way to road trip. In this section I'm going to do my best to include information to sites and services to set you in the right direction to find how you want to cater your experience.

Van Life: don't have the time, resousrces or money to convert your own van? Or do you just want to give van life a try before you buy? There is an incredible service called Escape Campervans that does just that. They have locations all around the U.S. and Canada and have multiole options to choose from to make your experince the best for you. That all being said, something like a campervan would be great to reduce prices of hotels and general lodging and you can van camp anywhere on this site for free!

Hotels: read the fine print and make sure you triple check locations in order to stay safe! Another great pointer is to book on the hotels actual website instead of through third party services (such as booking.com or expedia) since they do NOT have have first priority in room availability.

Parks: If you even consider going to 3 or more national parks, I would HIGHLY recommend purchasing an annual national park pass they are good for an entire year and will get you in to virtually every park without fee! Not only a great way to save money, but a great way to support the NPS!
